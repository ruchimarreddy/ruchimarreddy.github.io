About

I am currently pursuing my M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue University. My work focuses on understanding learning dynamics in deep neural networks, with particular interest in optimization behavior, generalization, and NTK-based analysis.

Research Interests

Deep Learning Theory

Studying principled explanations for training and generalization in overparameterized neural networks.

Optimization

Analyzing how optimization behaves across architectures and how theory compares with empirical training speed.

Neural Tangent Kernel

Exploring NTK alignment, eigenspectra, and label-kernel interactions in modern models.

Training Dynamics

Building experiments in PyTorch to study convergence, alignment growth, and layer-wise behavior.

Selected Projects

NTK Alignment Across Architectures

Empirical analysis of alignment trends in MLPs, CNNs, ResNets, and transformer-style models using PyTorch.

Layer-wise Training Dynamics

Developed pipelines to track per-layer alignment, gradient statistics, and update behavior during training.

Optimization Theory for Neural Networks

Compared worst-case spectral predictors with label-aware predictors to better explain observed convergence.

Machine Learning Experiments in PyTorch

Implemented research-oriented experiments across classification and regression settings for modern architectures.
